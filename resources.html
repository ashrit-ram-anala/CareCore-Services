Resources


Center for Disease Control and Prevention

The Center for Disease Control and Prevention is a world-renowned organization aimed to prevent diseases before they can attack. Specializing in vaccines and immunizations, the CDC lists yearly immunization schedules for every age group to make sure there are no doubts in how and when you should get your vaccine. In addition to a schedule, they also cover what vaccines each age group needs to make sure no one is left susceptible to an easily preventable disease. With their site being offered in several languages, their information is available to all.

Remote Area Medical

The Remote Area Medical Volunteer Corps is a nonprofit organization that is dedicated to providing free dental, vision, and medical services to those in underserved communities. They host free popup clinics comprised of professional practitioners and a support staff and with an easy to acces schedule on their site, finding a clinic near you is a prcess as simple as clicking a button. For those with travel restrictions, Remote Area Medical offers telehealth, a program where healthcare practitioners can care for patients through online appointments. No matter what, Remote Area Medical has you covered.

Medline Plus

Medline Plus is a online informational site under the National Institutes of Health that aims to provide high quality and relevant health and wellness information. Medline Plus includes easy to read and accurate health articles regarding various healthcare topics such as drugs and supplements, genetics, medical testing, and medical terms. Medline Plus also includes quick links to pages of medical professionals and covers medical information on every topic for every age group from children to older adults. In addition to medical information, Medline Plus has nutritional information as well, such as recipes for how to prepare a quick but nourishing meal.

Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services

The Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services offer healthcare information in 18 different languages, including Arabic, Armenian, Chinese, English, Farsi, French, German, Greek, Haitian Creole, Italian, Korean, Navajo, Polish, Portuguese, Russian, Spanish, Tagalog, and Vietnamese. They offer informative articles and infographics regarding insurance, coverage, and care, a newsroom discussing various medical news, a glossary to define all key medical terms, and a podcast to further spread medical information. On top of this, they also send out emails to update users on the latest news so information is right at their fingertips.

American Foundation for Suicide Prevention

The American Foundation for Suicide Prevention focuses on an aspect of healthcare that is often forgotten — mental health. They list mental health for the general population as well as mental health resources for various minority communities. With chapters spread through the Northeast United States, talking to someone who can help is made incredibly easy and convenient. Along with personal help, the organization also offers documentaries, classes, and presentations about how to be smarter with your mental health and shares stories of others in similar positions.

MCH Digital Library

The MCH Digital Library is a free and easy to access database of government, professional, and voluntary organizations that can aid in maternal and child health. With a total of 39 organizations to pick from, MCH Digital Library makes it simple to find an organization that bests suits your needs. ALong with organizations, MCH Digital Library also has a detailed list of family and professional resources to give advice in treating issues from asthma to teen violence, and also allows for the user to search through their database of established evidence to learn more about the subject.

National Association of Free & Charitable Clinics

The National Association of Free & Charitable Clinics makes finding an affordable clinic near you a simple and stress-free process. By simply entering your address and specify the distance, they can find clinics near you in a matter of seconds, making getting preventive care as easy as ever. They also offer blogs and events to further spread knowledge of healthcare topics such as nutrition, mental health, COVID-19, and many more. With over 1,400 free and charitable clinics in their database, 1.7 million patients served annually, and over 175 thousand volunteers, The National Association of Free & Charitable Clinics has your best needs at heart.

Testing

Testing is an online resource that lists and explains various tests you can consider to catch and prevent an issue from becoming much more severe in its early stages. From informtion regarding at-home testing to testing for serious diseases, Testing offers all the information you may need to get started on preventive care. Testing also aids in deciphering the results of a lab report and has a glossary of all the medical terms used so that a lack of medical knowledge is never an issue to get yourself tested. With a medical review board of trusted and knowledgeable professionals, you can be sure that Testing is giving you the best information.

Health Care For All

Health Care For All's Immigrant Health Toolkit is a resource that compiles information on issues that may be a barrier for immigrants accessing coverage and care. They cover information on a variety of topics such as COVID-19 FAQ, Public Charge Information (A program that allows immigrants to continue programs that they qualify for without having to worry about immigration repercussions), information about immigrants' right to health care, and a list of clinic locations divided up by region in the United States. Along with this, all of these resources are translated into multiple languages, making the Immigrant Health Toolkit an incredibly useful resource.
